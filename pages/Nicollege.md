# Nicollege/Auguniversity Box Fan

The **Nicollege/Auguniversity Box Fan**, commonly known as the **NAU Box Fan**, is an electric box fan associated with **Nicollege/Auguniversity**, a private university in the fictional U.S. state of **Provo**. Originally sold as a university-branded dormitory appliance, the fan became an unofficial symbol of the school and a recurring part of student traditions.

## History

Nicollege/Auguniversity was founded by actor and adventurer **Nicolas Cage**, who, according to university lore, established the institution shortly after stealing the Declaration of Independence. While the circumstances of this event remain the subject of debate and legend, the university credits Cage’s unconventional founding vision as the origin of its emphasis on independence, curiosity, and dramatic flair.

The Nicollege/Auguniversity Box Fan was introduced in 1998 following an unusually warm autumn semester in Provo. At the time, several of the university's older residence halls lacked central air conditioning. The Nicollege Office of Residential Comfort began purchasing inexpensive box fans for student housing, but shortages reportedly left several dormitories without adequate ventilation.

In 1999, the university entered into an agreement with Provo Air Movement Products, a regional appliance manufacturer, to produce a box fan specifically for Nicollege residence halls. The resulting fan featured a white plastic frame, a three-speed motor, and the university's navy-and-gold **N** emblem on the center grille.

The fan was initially university property and was checked out to students alongside dormitory keys and other residential equipment. Students frequently decorated the fans with stickers, residence hall names, and handwritten messages. By 2002, the university began selling branded versions through the campus bookstore.

During this same period, Nicollege/Auguniversity expanded its campus amenities with the establishment of the **Nicreamery**, a student-run ice cream and dairy shop intended to rival Brigham Young University's well-known creamery. Founded in 2001 after a group of students returned from a "research trip" to BYU with strong opinions about frozen desserts, the Nicreamery quickly became a central gathering place on campus. Known for its experimental flavors such as _Declaration Dough_ and _Cage Rage Chocolate_, the Nicreamery fostered a friendly but intense rivalry with BYU’s creamery. The competition occasionally extended into promotional events, including the 2005 "Scoops Showdown," where Nicollege students attempted to out-serve BYU in total ice cream volume over a 24-hour period. Though unofficial, the Nicreamery remains a beloved institution and is often mentioned alongside the box fan as a defining element of Nicollege student life.

## Design

The standard Nicollege/Auguniversity Box Fan is a 20-inch electric box fan. Most models use a white or off-white housing with navy and gold accents. The university name or **N** emblem is typically displayed at the center of the front grille.

The fan has traditionally included three speed settings and a built-in carrying handle. Several editions have been marketed with the slogan **"Powerful. Reliable. School Spirit."**

Despite frequent student claims that the highest setting is called **"Provo Mode,"** no officially released model has used the term on its controls.

## Campus traditions

The box fan developed a significant role in Nicollege/Auguniversity student culture during the early 2000s. Students commonly placed fans in residence hall windows, creating what the student newspaper described in 2004 as the **"Nicollege Wall of Wind."**

A tradition known as the **First Fan** occurs during the university's fall move-in period. According to the tradition, first-year students turn their box fans to their highest setting at 9:00 p.m. on the first Friday of the academic year. The practice has never been officially sponsored by the university.

Box fans are also frequently seen at outdoor athletic events, despite providing limited cooling in open-air environments. Students have defended the practice as a demonstration of school loyalty rather than a practical attempt at ventilation.

## The Great Fan Shortage

In 2011, Nicollege/Auguniversity experienced a widely publicized campus box fan shortage after an incoming first-year class significantly exceeded enrollment projections. The university bookstore reportedly sold its entire inventory of 1,400 fans within three days.

Students began trading used fans through campus message boards. According to the _Nicollege Daily Chronicle_, one unopened fan was offered in exchange for a used bicycle and two weeks of dining hall guest passes.

The event became known as the **Great Fan Shortage of 2011**. The university later increased its annual fan inventory and introduced a purchase limit during move-in week.

## Controversies

The fan has occasionally been the subject of minor campus controversies. In 2016, university housing officials prohibited students from operating more than four box fans simultaneously in a single dormitory room after a group of engineering students constructed what they described as a **"wind tunnel."** The university stated that the structure blocked an emergency exit and repeatedly tripped a circuit breaker.

In 2021, students petitioned the university to make the box fan the official school mascot. The proposal received 3,802 student signatures but was rejected by the university's Board of Trustees. The university retained the **Provo Golden Eagle** as its official mascot.

## Cultural impact

The Nicollege/Auguniversity Box Fan is widely associated with dormitory life at the university. Miniature fan replicas, stickers, shirts, and novelty desk fans have been sold by student organizations and local businesses throughout Provo.

The phrase **"Go Nicollege, Blow Nicollege"** originated as a student joke referencing the fan and has occasionally appeared on unofficial merchandise. The university does not formally recognize the slogan.

In 2024, Nicollege/Auguniversity released a commemorative box fan celebrating what it described as 25 years of university-branded fan production. The model used a navy carrying handle and a gold-trimmed university emblem.
